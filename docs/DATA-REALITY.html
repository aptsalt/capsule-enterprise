CAPSULE · Data Honesty

What's real vs mock

Hackathon integrity matters. This is the exact, honest line between what CAPSULE actually computes and stores on your machine and the small slice that is still derived scaffolding. As of this build, most of CAPSULE is real — the seeded Meridian scenario has been replaced by measured data, scoring is now LLM-judged (not a trained benchmark), and the eval is a multi-sample measured proxy.

One-line answer

✓ The dataset and the loop are real

The capsules (CAP-R001…R008 plus the self-captured CAP-SESSION-1a6fcc9b) are distilled from your real ~/.claude sessions by local Ollama qwen2.5-coder:14b (chunked map-reduce over the whole session), scored by an LLM judge (scoreCapsuleLLM + noveltyLLM, heuristic fallback), gated, and written to live Backboard memory with real thread_ids. The enterprise registry holds 28 skills (13 real + 15 popular); the end-of-day promote opens CI-gated PRs into master (dee's two upgrades, the self-captured ui-modularity@1.0.0). The A/B eval shows measured, multi-sample Ollama token deltas; metrics are computed from the real capsule data. The workspace was rebranded Meridian → CAPSULE.

~ A thin layer is still derived

Per-capsule reuses, scoreDelta/adoptedBy, the non-A/B per-capsule tokensSaved estimates (reuses × per-reuse), and the requirements/workOrders objects remain authored scaffolding. Scoring is LLM-judged, not a trained model, and the eval is a measured proxy, not production telemetry — honest framings, stated plainly. Everything else below is measured or live.
